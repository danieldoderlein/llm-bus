# Legal review notes (before real-money launch)

These ToS/Privacy/Pricing drafts are thin and founder-drafted. Have a Norwegian lawyer confirm the
following before taking real payments. The first two are the load-bearing ones.

1. **Credit forfeiture (12-month inactivity).** Forfeiting prepaid, paid-for balances can run into
   Norwegian/EU consumer-protection rules (angrerettloven / Consumer Rights Directive, prepaid-value
   rules). For consumers especially, "use it or lose it" on money already paid is the clause most
   likely to be challenged. Confirm enforceability + whether pre-forfeiture notice is required.
2. **Consumer vs business + right of withdrawal.** If any owners are consumers, the 14-day withdrawal
   right and pre-contract disclosures apply. Likely want a clause where the consumer consents to
   immediate performance and waives withdrawal once credits are consumed.
3. **Liability cap (3 months' fees).** May not hold against consumers or for gross negligence/intent
   under Norwegian law. Confirm survival + add standard carve-outs.
4. **"As is" / no-warranty.** Norwegian mandatory consumer law limits warranty disclaimers to
   consumers. Fine B2B, weaker B2C - confirm scope.
5. **VAT position.** Prices stated tax-inclusive while under the threshold. Confirm the threshold,
   when registration becomes mandatory, and that cross-border digital-services VAT to EU/EEA
   customers doesn't trigger regardless of the domestic threshold.
6. **"May change billable actions and rates at any time."** Unilateral change clauses are scrutinised
   for consumers; the "announced, not retroactive" framing helps. Confirm notice + any
   right-to-cancel-on-change requirement.
7. **GDPR ledger-retention disclosure.** "Deleting a participant keeps the name on the ledger" is a
   genuine tension with the right to erasure - defensible (legitimate interest / record integrity)
   but confirm the legal basis, the disclosure's sufficiency, and a documented true-erasure process.
